Tuitt Training Bootcamp sample webpages

This page contains various sample activities accomplished during my Tuitt training bootcamp. These where my exercises before learning the different Works of Arts in a webpage. These were my baby steps to be Front-end Developer. The learning curve is not as steep and surely everyone can it. What makes webpage design is when it viewed as an ART.

HTML, CSS, and Design

  • Before I enrolled at Tuitt, this is all that I knew...
  • I am complacent that front-end web development is easy...
  • Yeah it was easy, as easy as pie...
  • And then we were tasked with this weird, Reverse-Engineering-type of activity... It was a DOM-related activity... That activity hit my brain cells! It made me wonder if I could really make it... But on the other side, I am fascinated on how coding is organized from tons of elements inside an HTML, I think it is analoguous to complex fractions wherein elements are grouped around.
  • I know.... it is ugly.... Don't laugh!
  • Everytime I sat on my workstation and start coding, I began to see the importance of every pixels on the viewport. The tiny 1px overflow at border is visible and ultimately makes your art ugly (unless overflow is intentional). The topics that lectured to us made me realize that we are doing an art sessions.. My monitor is a canvas, and my editor is my palette.
  • Don't get me wrong, I have no natural talent for arts. In the field of Arts, I am just a mere observer and a listener.
  • But hey! There's nothing really to lose if I go further...
